Explanation:
Because Saint Martin’s is an offshore island in the Bay of Bengal, visitors must travel by boat or launch from the mainland, so there are no direct road or rail routes. This sea-only access, plus reliance on weather and tidal conditions, makes it more remote and gives it a distinctive, less crowded appeal.
