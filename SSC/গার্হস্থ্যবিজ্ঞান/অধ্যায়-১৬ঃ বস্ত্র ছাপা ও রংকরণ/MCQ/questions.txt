Solution:
Solution:
Step 1 — সংজ্ঞা: ধরুন শূন্য অনুমান হবে [math]H_0[/math]. p‑value হল শূন্য অনুমানের নিচে "অন্তত ততটা চরম" তথ্য পাওয়ার সম্ভাব্যতা।  
Step 2 — গাণিতিক প্রকাশ: যদি টেস্ট স্ট্যাটিস্টিক হয় [math]T[/math] এবং পর্যবেক্ষিত মান [math]T_{\text{obs}}[/math], তাহলে সাধারণভাবে [math]p = P(T \ge T_{\text{obs}}\mid H_0)[/math] (দুই-পাশে হলে [math]p = 2P(T \ge |T_{\text{obs}}|\mid H_0)[/math])।  
Step 3 — ব্যাখ্যা ও সিদ্ধান্ত: ছোট p‑value মানে পর্যবেক্ষিত ফল সাধারণ অনুভবযোগ্য নয় কেবলমাত্র সুযোগবশত ঘটে যাওয়ার সম্ভাবনা কম — তাই আমরা [math]H_0[/math] প্রত্যাখ্যান করার দিকে অগ্রসর হতে পারি; বড় p‑value মানে ডেটা শূন্য অনুমানের সাথে সামঞ্জস্যপূর্ণ।  
তথ্যগত সতর্কতা: p‑value কখনই সরাসরি শূন্য অনুমানের সত্যের সম্ভাব্যতা নয় এবং না এটি প্রভাবের আকার নির্দেশ করে।

Question:
What is a longitudinal study?

Options:


Correct Answer: A study that follows the same subjects over a long period of time.

Solution:
Solution:
A longitudinal study is one that selects a group of subjects (a cohort) and collects repeated observations or measurements on those same individuals over an extended period. The key methodological steps are: choose the cohort, measure the variables of interest at multiple time points, and analyze within-subject changes over time — this design directly matches the statement “follows the same subjects over a long period of time.” Other common designs (e.g., cross-sectional studies) take a single-time “snapshot” of different subjects and thus do not follow the same individuals over time, so those would be incorrect.

Question:
What is a confounding variable?

Options:


Correct Answer: An outside influence that can affect the outcome of an experiment.

Solution:
Solution: A confounding variable is a third factor that is related to both the independent variable and the dependent variable and thus can create a false impression of a causal relationship. 

Step 1: Identify the independent and dependent variables in the study.  
Step 2: Check for any outside factor that varies with both and could influence the outcome — that factor is the confounder.  
Step 3: Recognize that this outside influence can bias results unless controlled (e.g., by randomization, stratification, or statistical adjustment).

Reasoning: The correct answer, "An outside influence that can affect the outcome of an experiment," matches the definition because a confounder is not part of the planned treatment but still affects the observed effect. Other choices (for example, “measurement error” or “random variation”) are different problems: measurement error affects data accuracy, and random variation is natural variability — neither specifically creates a spurious association between independent and dependent variables like a confounder does.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Question:
What is the purpose of randomization in experiments?

Options:


Correct Answer: To eliminate bias and ensure that the groups are comparable.

Solution:
Solution:
1. Define the goal: Randomization means assigning subjects to treatment or control groups by a chance mechanism so that assignment is independent of participants’ characteristics.  
2. Method and effect: By random assignment each known and unknown confounding factor has (on average) the same distribution across groups, which removes systematic differences (bias) between groups and makes them comparable.  
3. Conclusion: Therefore the primary purpose of randomization is to eliminate bias and ensure groups are comparable, allowing observed outcome differences to be attributed to the treatment rather than preexisting differences.  

Why other options are incorrect (brief): Randomization does not increase sample size or guarantee identical groups in every sample (only balance on average), nor does it create effects — it only prevents selection bias so causal inference is valid.

Question:
What is bias in scientific research?

Options:


Correct Answer: A systematic error that affects the results of a study.

Solution:
Solution:
Step 1: Define bias — bias is a systematic error in the design, conduct, or analysis of a study that consistently pushes the estimated effect away from the true value.  
Step 2: Reasoning — because it is systematic, bias affects results in a predictable direction (overestimate or underestimate) and does not average out with larger sample size.  
Step 3: How to identify/control — check for selection bias, information (measurement) bias, and analytical bias; use proper randomization, blinding, standardized measurements, and adjusted analyses to reduce bias.  
Why other options are incorrect: random error (chance) causes unpredictable variability that decreases with larger samples, and confounding is distortion by a third variable; neither is the same as a systematic error that consistently skews results.  

Therefore the correct answer is: a systematic error that affects the results of a study.

Question:
What is the importance of sample size in research?

Options:


Correct Answer: Larger sample sizes generally lead to more reliable and valid results.

Solution:
Solution:
Step 1 — বোঝাপড়া: sample size (নমুনা আকার) হলো গবেষণায় নিযুক্ত পর্যবেক্ষণের সংখ্যা; এটি অনুমানগুলোর নির্ভরযোগ্যতা ও স্থায়িত্বকে প্রভাবিত করে।  
Step 2 — পরিমিতি ও সূত্র: মানদণ্ডগত অনিশ্চয়তার প্রধান উপাদান হলো standard error, যা সাধারণত [math]SE = \sigma/\sqrt{n}[/math] দ্বারা প্রকাশ করা হয়; এখানে [math]\sigma[/math] হলো জনসংখ্যার মানদণ্ড এবং [math]n[/math] হলো নমুনা আকার। বড় [math]n[/math] হলে [math]SE[/math] ছোট হয়, ফলে অনুমানগুলি বেশি সঠিক হয়।  
Step 3 — অর্থ ও প্রভাব: Confidence interval-এর অর্ধ-প্রস্থ [math](\text{half-width}) = z_{\alpha/2}\times SE[/math] হওয়ায় বড় নমুনা আকার সংকোচিত বিস্তারের কারণ হয়ে ওঠে এবং পরীক্ষার শক্তি (statistical power) বেড়ে যায়।  
Step 4 — সীমাবদ্ধতা ও যুক্তি: বৃহৎ নমুনা সাধারণত আরও নির্ভরযোগ্য ফল দেয়, কিন্তু খরচ ও সময় বাড়ে এবং লাভ মনোযোগযোগ্যভাবে কমে কারণ [math]SE[/math] কমে যায় কেবলমাত্র [math]\sqrt{n}[/math] অনুযায়ী (ডবল করলে [math]SE[/math] হয় ≈[math]1/\sqrt{2}[/math] গুণ)।  
নिष্কর্ষ: তাই সঠিক উত্তর—“Larger sample sizes generally lead to more reliable and valid results.” ছোট নমুনা নির্বাচন করলে sampling error বেশি হয় এবং ফলাফল কম নির্ভরযোগ্য হবে; অতিরিক্তভাবে অত্যন্ত বড় নমুনা ছাড়া অন্যান্য বিকল্প (যেমন খরচবহুল কিন্তু অপ্রয়োজনীয় অতিরিক্ত নমুনা) কার্যকর নয়।

Question:
What is a scientific law?

Options:


Correct Answer: A statement based on repeated experimental observations that describes an aspect of the world.

Solution:
Solution:
Step 1: Identify the defining feature of a scientific law — it is a concise statement that summarizes consistent, repeatable observations from experiments or nature.  
Step 2: Contrast with similar terms: a law describes what happens (regular patterns), whereas a theory explains why those patterns occur; a hypothesis is a tentative proposal, not a repeatedly verified summary.  
Step 3: Therefore the correct choice is the statement that a scientific law is "a statement based on repeated experimental observations that describes an aspect of the world." Other options that call a law a mere guess, a full explanation, or a moral rule are incorrect because they do not reflect the empirical, descriptive, and repeatable nature of a scientific law.

Question:
What does it mean to replicate a study?

Options:


Correct Answer: To conduct the same study again to verify results.

Solution:
Solution:
Step 1 — Define: To replicate a study means to conduct the same study again, following the original methods, sample characteristics, measurements, and analysis to see if the same findings emerge.  
Step 2 — Methodology: Replication involves reproducing the procedures (experimental protocol, data collection, and statistical tests) as closely as possible and then comparing the new results to the original results.  
Step 3 — Reasoning: This verifies whether the original result was reliable or possibly due to chance, bias, or error. Other choices (for example, merely reanalyzing the same dataset, publishing again, or intentionally changing key procedures) are not replication because they do not repeat the study in the same way to confirm the original outcome.

Question:
What is a theory in scientific terms?

Options:


Correct Answer: A well-substantiated explanation of an aspect of the natural world.

Solution:
Solution:
Step 1: Identify the scientific meaning of "theory" — it is more than a simple guess; it is an explanation that integrates and accounts for a wide range of observations and experimental results.  
Step 2: Note the phrase "well-substantiated" — this means the explanation is supported by extensive evidence and repeated testing, not a single untested idea.  
Step 3: Distinguish from related terms: a hypothesis is a tentative proposal to be tested, a scientific law describes a consistent pattern or relationship (often mathematically) but does not explain why, and a model is a simplified representation; a theory explains the underlying mechanisms.  
Therefore the correct answer is: a well-substantiated explanation of an aspect of the natural world.

Question:
What does peer review ensure in scientific research?

Options:


Correct Answer: The quality and validity of research before publication.

Solution:
Step 1: Peer review is the process in which independent experts evaluate a manuscript's methods, data, analysis, and conclusions before a journal publishes it.  
Step 2: Reviewers check for sound experimental or analytical design, reproducibility, appropriate statistics, logical interpretation, and ethical compliance—so errors, bias, or unsupported claims can be identified and corrected.  
Step 3: Because of these checks, peer review ensures the quality and validity of research prior to publication.  
Step 4: Other options (for example, that peer review guarantees novelty, speeds up publication, or advances an author's career) are incorrect because peer review assesses rigor and validity rather than guaranteeing originality, rapid turnaround, or career outcomes.

Question:
What is the scientific method?

Options:


Correct Answer: A systematic process for investigating phenomena and acquiring new knowledge.

Solution:
Solution:
The scientific method is a systematic, evidence-based process for investigating phenomena and acquiring new knowledge. Typical steps are: (1) make observations, (2) ask a clear question, (3) propose a testable hypothesis, (4) derive predictions, (5) perform controlled experiments or gather data, (6) analyze results and draw conclusions, and (7) refine or repeat as needed and communicate findings. This sequence shows why the correct answer is “a systematic process for investigating phenomena and acquiring new knowledge”: it emphasizes structure, testability, reproducibility, and evidence rather than opinion. Incorrect views — for example, that science is just a single experiment, untested guess, or mere collection of facts — fail because they lack hypothesis testing, controls, and reproducibility.

Question:
What is the purpose of a control group in an experiment?

Options:


Correct Answer: To provide a baseline for comparison with the experimental group.

Solution:
Solution:
1. A control group is the group in an experiment that does not receive the experimental treatment; it is kept under normal or baseline conditions.  
2. Method: keep all conditions the same between groups except the independent variable (the treatment). The experimental group receives the treatment while the control group does not.  
3. Reasoning: by comparing outcomes of the experimental group to the control group, researchers can attribute any difference to the treatment rather than to other factors. This provides a baseline for comparison, which is the purpose of the control group.  
4. Why other options are incorrect (brief): options like “to receive the treatment” or “to produce the expected result” are wrong because the control group’s role is not to get the treatment or guarantee outcomes but to serve as the untreated baseline for valid comparison.
