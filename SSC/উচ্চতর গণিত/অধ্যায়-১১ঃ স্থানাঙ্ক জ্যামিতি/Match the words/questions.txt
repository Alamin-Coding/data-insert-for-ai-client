Question:
What is the significance of the slope of a line in determining its equation and how does it relate to the coordinates of specific points on the line?

Options:


Correct Answer: The slope determines how steep the line is, affecting its equation based on point coordinates.

Solution:


Explanation:
The slope measures how steep the line is and gives the rate at which y changes with x, so knowing the slope and one point lets you write the line's equation. The slope between two points is [math]m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}[/math], and the line is [math]y = mx + b[/math] where [math]b[/math] is found by substituting a known point.

Question:
What geometric shape is formed by plotting points in a coordinate plane?

Options:
a. Circle
b. Triangle
c. Polygon
d. Square


Correct Answer: গ

Solution:
Solution:
Step 1: When you plot a finite set of points in the coordinate plane and join them in sequence by straight line segments, you form a closed figure whose sides are straight segments — that is a polygon.  
Step 2: Specific shapes like a triangle or a square are just special types of polygons (triangle = 3-sided polygon, square = 4-sided regular polygon). For example, the points [math](0,0),\ (1,0),\ (1,1),\ (0,1)[/math] joined in order form a polygon (a square).  
Therefore the most general and correct choice is option c: Polygon.  
Other options: a circle is not a polygon because its boundary is curved, and b (triangle) and d (square) are only specific polygons, not the general case.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Question:
What does the area of a triangle depend on?

Options:
a. The length of its sides only.
b. The height and base values.
c. The angles of the triangle.
d. The perimeter of the triangle.


Correct Answer: খ

Solution:
Solution:
The area of a triangle is determined by its base and the corresponding height (altitude). The standard formula is [math]A=\tfrac{1}{2}\times\text{base}\times\text{height}[/math], so knowing the base and its perpendicular height gives the area directly.

Why other options are incorrect or incomplete:
- Option a: Knowing side lengths can allow computation of area via Heron's formula [math]A=\sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}[/math], but this is an indirect method; the elementary and direct dependency is on a base and its height.  
- Option c: Angles alone do not fix the area because similar triangles have identical angles but can have different sizes (if linear scale factor is [math]k[/math], area scales by [math]k^2[/math]).  
- Option d: Perimeter alone does not determine area, since different-shaped triangles can share the same perimeter but have different areas.

Therefore the correct answer is b.

Question:
How do you define the midpoint of a line segment in coordinate geometry?

Options:
a. As the average of the coordinates of the endpoints.
b. As the sum of the coordinates of the endpoints.
c. As the largest coordinate value.
d. As the smallest coordinate value.


Correct Answer: ক

Solution:
Solution:
The midpoint M of a segment with endpoints [math](x_1,y_1)[/math] and [math](x_2,y_2)[/math] is found by averaging the corresponding coordinates: [math]M\!\left(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2},\frac{y_1+y_2}{2}\right)[/math]. This formula gives the point exactly halfway because each coordinate is the arithmetic mean of the two endpoint coordinates, so M is equidistant from both endpoints. Hence option a is correct. Option b is incorrect because taking the sum [math](x_1+x_2,y_1+y_2)[/math] does not place the point halfway (it must be halved); options c and d are irrelevant because the midpoint is not simply the largest or smallest coordinate.

Question:
What is the formula for the area of a triangle using its base and height?

Options:
a. Area = base × height
b. Area = 1/2 × base × height
c. Area = base + height
d. Area = base - height


Correct Answer: খ

Solution:
Solution:
Step 1: The standard formula for the area of a triangle with base [math]b[/math] and height [math]h[/math] is [math]A = \tfrac{1}{2} b h[/math].

Step 2: Reason: a triangle with base [math]b[/math] and height [math]h[/math] fits exactly as half of a rectangle of area [math]b \times h[/math], so its area is half of that, i.e. [math]\tfrac{1}{2}bh[/math].

Step 3: Therefore the correct choice is option (b). Options (a) omits the necessary factor of [math]\tfrac{1}{2}[/math]; (c) and (d) use addition/subtraction which are not formulas for area.
