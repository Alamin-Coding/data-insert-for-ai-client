Question:
P={x:x2−9=0},Q={x:x2+x−6=0}P={x:x2−9=0},Q={x:x2+x−6=0}P=\left\{x: x^2-9=0\right\}, Q=\left\{x: x^2+x-6=0\right\} হলে P∩Q=P∩Q=P \cap Q= কত?

Options:
ক. $\{-3,2,3\}$
খ. $\{-3\}$
গ. $\{2\}$
ঘ. $\{3\}$


Correct Answer: খ

Solution:
$\mathrm{P}=\left\{\mathrm{x}: \mathrm{x}^2-9=0\right\}$
এখন, $x^2-9=0$ বা, $x^2=9$
$\therefore x= \pm 3 \therefore P=\{-3,3\}$
এবং $Q=\left\{x: x^2+x-6=0\right\}$
$x^2+x-6=0$ বা, $x^2+3 x-2 x-6=0$
বা, $x(x+3)-2(x+3)=0$ বা, $(x+3)(x-2)=0$
$\therefore x=2,-3 \therefore Q=\{-3,2\}$
$\therefore P \cap Q=\{-3,3\} \cap\{-3,2\}=\{-3\}$.
