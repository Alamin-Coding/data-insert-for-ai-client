Solution:
23 January 2025

To

The Headmaster

Kabi Nazrul High School

Mymensingh

Explanation:
Load shedding refers to the planned, temporary interruption of electrical power supply to certain areas to prevent a total collapse of the power grid when demand exceeds available generation. It is typically used as an emergency measure when generation capacity, fuel supply, transmission constraints, or maintenance shortfalls make it impossible to meet peak demand. The immediate effects are widespread: households lose lighting and refrigeration, businesses halt production, schools and hospitals face disruptions, and public safety can be compromised. Repeated outages erode productivity, increase costs for businesses (through lost work and damaged equipment), and reduce the quality of life for residents.

Root causes include inadequate generation capacity, aging infrastructure, fuel shortages, poor maintenance, financial problems in power utilities, and weak grid management. Short-term coping strategies include scheduled outage notices, rotating supply to distribute inconvenience, backup generators, and community cooling/storage centers. Long-term solutions focus on increasing and diversifying supply (building new plants, investing in renewables like solar and wind), improving grid infrastructure and maintenance, strengthening transmission lines, implementing demand-side management (energy efficiency, time-of-use tariffs, smart meters), and reforming utility finances and governance to ensure reliable fuel procurement and investment.

Effective policies combine technical upgrades with realistic planning and public communication so outages are minimized and better managed. Encouraging rooftop solar, battery storage, and microgrids can reduce peak pressure on the central grid and provide resilience for critical services.
