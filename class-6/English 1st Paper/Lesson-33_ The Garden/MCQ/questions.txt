Question:
How do the definitions of hygiene and personal health practices differ, and why is each important for maintaining good health?

Options:
a. Hygiene refers to cleanliness, while personal health practices involve nutrition; both are crucial for health.
b. Hygiene is about diet, while personal health practices are about exercise; only diet is essential for health.
c. Hygiene includes physical health, while personal health practices are limited to mental health; both are equally unimportant.
d. Hygiene is irrelevant, but personal health practices are essential for maintaining physical health.


Correct Answer: ক

Solution:
Solution:
Hygiene refers to practices that maintain cleanliness and prevent infection (e.g., handwashing, bathing, sanitation), while personal health practices cover broader behaviors that sustain health such as nutrition, exercise, sleep, and preventive care. Both are important because hygiene reduces exposure to pathogens and immediate illness, and personal health practices support long-term physical and mental well‑being. Option (a) correctly contrasts hygiene (cleanliness) with personal health practices (including nutrition) and notes that both are crucial. Options (b), (c), and (d) are incorrect because they mischaracterize the terms (mixing up diet, exercise, mental vs. physical health) or dismiss the importance of hygiene.
