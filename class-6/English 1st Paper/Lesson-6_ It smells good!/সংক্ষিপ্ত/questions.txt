Question:
What connections can be drawn between the themes of sacrifice in the Liberation War and Suraiya Begum's desire to uplift lonely elderly individuals in her community?

Options:


Correct Answer: Both highlight the importance of caring for others and recognizing their sacrifices.

Solution:


Explanation:
Both themes emphasize selfless care and valuing others’ contributions: the Liberation War shows large-scale sacrifice for the common good, while Suraiya’s work translates that same ethic into everyday care for lonely elders. In both cases the message is to recognize people’s sacrifices, honor their dignity, and respond with tangible support.

Question:
In what ways do Suraiya Begum's intentions to establish an old people's home demonstrate the importance of community support, similar to how the nation honors its martyrs on Victory Day?

Options:


Correct Answer: Both emphasize the need for care and support for vulnerable populations.

Solution:


Explanation:
Both are about showing collective care and support for vulnerable groups: honoring martyrs on Victory Day recognizes their sacrifice and unites the nation in respect, while establishing an old people’s home provides practical care and dignity for elderly people. The underlying concept is social responsibility and solidarity—society must both honor and actively support those who need recognition or care.
