Question:
[math]\int_{0}^{\pi / 2} \cos ^{4} \theta \sin \theta d \theta=?[/math]

Options:


Correct Answer: খ

Solution:
[math]\text { Shortcut: Calculator }[/math]

Explanation:
Use substitution [math]u=\cos\theta,\;du=-\sin\theta\,d\theta[/math], so [math]\int_0^{\pi/2}\cos^4\theta\sin\theta\,d\theta=-\int_1^0 u^4\,du=\int_0^1 u^4\,du=[u^5/5]_0^1=1/5[/math], hence option B is correct.
