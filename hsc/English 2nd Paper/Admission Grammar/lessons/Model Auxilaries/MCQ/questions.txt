Mr. Atique — rather not invest that money in the stock market.

Options:
A. must
B. has to
C. could
D. would


Correct Answer: ঘ

Solution:

Explanation:

Has to দিয়ে অন্যের দ্বারা নিজের উপর আরোপিত বাধ্যবাধকতা বোঝায় ।

এখানে বাক্যে কোন বাধ্যবাধকতা বা শর্ত বুঝায় নি । তাই প্রারম্ভিকভাবে would ব্যবহৃত হবে । তাই, would rather modal auxiliary-টি হবে সঠিক উত্তর ।

Which of the following pairs are semi-modal verbs?

Options:

b. should, must
c. can, could
d. will, shall


Correct Answer: a

Solution:
Option (a) is correct because 'ought to' and 'need' are classified as semi-modal (or marginal modal) verbs — they behave like modals in many ways but differ in form (e.g., 'ought' takes 'to', 'need' can act as a full verb or a semi-modal). The other pairs (b, c, d) are standard modal auxiliaries.

In contrast, 'should, must, can, could, will, shall' are core modals with typical modal behavior (no infinitive marker, no -s in the third person, etc.).

Question:
The teacher recommended that every student -------- the assignment by Friday.

Options:
a. submit
b. submits
c. not submit
d. submitted


Correct Answer: a

Solution:
According to the subjunctive rule: recommend + that + subject + base form of the verb. Here 'recommended that every student submit' uses the base form 'submit'.

Explanation:
When verbs like recommend, suggest, insist, demand are used with 'that' clauses, English uses the subjunctive: the verb after the subject stays in its base form (submit). 'Submits' is present indicative, 'submitted' is past, and 'not submit' is unnecessary negative form here.

Question:
You should — the instructions carefully

Options:
a. follow
b. to follow
c. followed
d. following


Correct Answer: a

Solution:
Use the base form 'follow' after the modal verb 'should'.

Explanation:
'Should' is a modal auxiliary verb; after modal verbs we use the base form (bare infinitive) of the verb. 'Follow' is the base form, so option (a) is correct. 'To follow' is the full infinitive and not used after modals, 'followed' is past participle, and 'following' is the gerund/participle — both are incorrect here.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Question:
She ------ driving in heavy traffic now.

Options:
ক. used to drive
খ. is used to drive
গ. is used to driving
ঘ. used to driving


Correct Answer: গ

Solution:
ব্যক্তিবাচক Subject এর পরে যদি অতীতের অভ্যাস বোঝাতে হয় তবে 'used to' এর পরে verb এর base form ব্যবহার হয় (উদাহরণ: used to drive)। কিন্তু 'be used to' মানে 'অভ্যস্ত' এবং এর পরে noun বা verb-ing লাগে। তাই বর্তমান অবস্থা বোঝাতে সঠিক হয় 'is used to driving'।

Explanation:
প্রতিটি অপশনের বিশ্লেষণ:
- ক (used to drive): অতীতের অভ্যাস বোঝায়—'She used to drive' মানে আগে তার অভ্যাস ছিল কিন্তু এখানে বাক্যে 'now' ব্যবহার হয়েছে, তাই সঠিক নয়।
- খ (is used to drive): 'be used to' এর পরে base form আসে না—ভুল রূপ।
- গ (is used to driving): সঠিক—'be used to' + verb-ing ব্যবহার করে 'অভ্যস্ত' অর্থ প্রকাশ করা হয়।
- ঘ (used to driving): 'used to' + ing থাকে না; এটি ভ্রান্ত গঠন।
