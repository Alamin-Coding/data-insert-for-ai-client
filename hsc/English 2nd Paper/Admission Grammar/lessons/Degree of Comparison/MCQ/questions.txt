Question:
More ivory is obtained from elephants in Africa ---- elephant in Asia.

Options:
A. than from
B. as well as
C. rather than
D. more than


Correct Answer: ক

Solution:
Rather than usually occurs between two things which are being compared. However, we can also use it at the beginning of a sentence. When we use rather than with a verb, we use the base form or (less commonly) the - ing form of a verb: Rather than pay the taxi fare, he walked home.

Explanation:
উল্লিখিত প্রশ্নে আফ্রিকান এবং এশিয়ান হাতির তুলনা করতে than from ব্যবহার করলেই বাক্যটি সঠিক অর্থ প্রকাশ করে ।

Question:
Which of the following is incorrect?

Options:
A. Tallest
B. Largest
C. Biggest
D. Hugest
E. সঠিক উত্তর নেই


Correct Answer: E. সঠিক উত্তর নেই

Solution:
All of the words - biggest, largest, tallest, and hugest - are correct in most contexts. They are all synonyms that can mean "of the greatest size or amount."

However, "hugest" is a bit informal compared to the others. While it's perfectly understandable, "largest" or "biggest" might be a better choice for formal writing.

So, technically, none of the options are incorrect, but "hugest" is the least formal option on the list.
