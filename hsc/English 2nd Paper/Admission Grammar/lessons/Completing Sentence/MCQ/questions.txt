Question:
Please ------------- your dog when visitors come.

Options:
a. stop
b. restrain
c. prevent
d. ignore


Correct Answer: খ

Solution:
Please restrain your dog — keep your dog under control when visitors arrive.

Explanation:
‘Restrain’ means to keep the dog under control or hold it back, which is appropriate when visitors come. ‘Stop’ and ‘prevent’ are less natural here (they imply halting an action rather than controlling the animal), and ‘ignore’ is the opposite of what is needed.

Question:
Can you ---------- me your notes for the lecture? I missed the class.

Options:
a. lend
b. borrow
c. hire
d. take


Correct Answer: ক

Solution:
When someone gives something to another person temporarily, we use 'lend'. The speaker is asking the other person to give their notes temporarily.

Explanation:
'Lend' means to give something to someone for a short time. 'Borrow' means to take something from someone for a short time (you borrow from someone). 'Hire' means to pay to use something or employ someone. 'Take' is more general and doesn't specify the temporary lending relationship.

Question:
Although she had little formal schooling, her keen powers of observation and persistent curiosity made her an unusually ------ woman.

Options:
a. humble
b. sagacious
c. obstinate
d. impulsive


Correct Answer: খ

Solution:
humble - modest; sagacious - wise, showing good judgment; obstinate - stubborn; impulsive - acting without forethought. The best fit is 'sagacious'.

Explanation:
The sentence contrasts limited formal schooling with mental qualities that produced a notable outcome. 'Keen powers of observation and persistent curiosity' lead to wisdom and good judgment, so 'sagacious' (wise) is the appropriate choice. The other options do not match the positive intellectual result: 'humble' refers to modesty, 'obstinate' to stubbornness, and 'impulsive' to rash behavior.

Question:
She applied for a personal ------- to pay for her unexpected medical bills.

Options:
a. loan
b. credit
c. debt
d. mortgage


Correct Answer: ক

Solution:
She applied for a personal loan — a sum of money borrowed from a bank or lender that must be repaid, usually with interest.

Explanation:
A 'loan' is the correct choice because it specifically refers to borrowed money obtained to cover expenses. 'Credit' can refer to an arrangement to borrow but is more general (e.g., a credit card or line of credit); 'debt' is the state of owing money rather than the instrument used to obtain funds; 'mortgage' is a loan secured by property, typically for buying real estate, not usually used for medical bills.

Question:
His __________ lifestyle explains why he is constantly strapped for cash.

Options:
a. abstemious
b. profligate
c. moderate
d. parsimonious


Correct Answer: খ

Solution:
Profligate means recklessly extravagant or wasteful in the use of resources (synonyms: extravagant, spendthrift, wasteful).

Explanation:
The sentence indicates excessive spending causing lack of money. 'Profligate' fits this meaning. 'Abstemious' means self-disciplined or moderate in consumption (opposite meaning). 'Moderate' also implies not excessive. 'Parsimonious' means extremely frugal or stingy, which would not explain being short of cash due to overspending.
