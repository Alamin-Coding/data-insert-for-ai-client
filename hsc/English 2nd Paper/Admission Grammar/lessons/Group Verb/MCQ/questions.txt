c. an adverbial phrase
d. a noun phrase


Correct Answer: ঘ

Solution:

Question:
The crew abandoned the ship because it -------

Options:
a. turned down
b. turned up
c. turned over
d. turned away


Correct Answer: গ

Solution:
Turn over phrase - means to capsize or flip. "Turned down" means to refuse; "turned up" means to appear; "turned away" means to refuse entry or to move aside.

Explanation:
The crew abandoned the ship because the ship capsized. The correct phrasal verb for a vessel flipping is "turn over." Other options do not convey sinking or capsizing.

Question:
Several preliminary examinations were _____ before the operation.

Options:
a. carried out
b. put off
c. made up
d. gone through


Correct Answer: ক

Solution:
"carried out" is the appropriate phrasal verb meaning 'performed' or 'conducted'. In passive voice we say 'were carried out'.

Explanation:
"Put off" means postponed, which doesn't fit here. "Made up" means invented or composed. "Gone through" can mean examined or experienced, but the common collocation for conducting examinations or tests is "carried out", so the correct answer is "carried out".

Question:
"After the university introduced online registration, the new course took off quickly among freshmen" - In this sentence 'took off' refers to ----------.

Options:
a. becoming successful or popular quickly
b. being physically removed or taken away
c. an airplane starting its flight
d. a sudden decrease in enrollment


Correct Answer: ক

Solution:
Here 'took off' means that the course became popular or successful very quickly after online registration was introduced.

Explanation:
The phrase 'took off' commonly means to become successful, popular, or widely adopted in a short time. Context words like 'quickly' and 'among freshmen' indicate rising popularity. Option b (being removed) and option c (airplane departure) are other literal meanings of 'take off' but do not fit this context. Option d is the opposite meaning and therefore incorrect.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Question:
The house was broken ______ by burglars.

Options:
a. over
b. into
c. up
d. off


Correct Answer: খ

Solution:
'break into' is the phrasal verb meaning to enter a place by force. In passive voice it becomes 'was broken into by burglars' — so 'into' is correct.

Explanation:
'break into' = to enter (a building) illegally or by force. 'The burglars broke into the house' → passive: 'The house was broken into by burglars.' 'Over', 'up', and 'off' do not form the correct phrasal verb with 'break' meaning to enter a place.

Question:
The committee will look into the matter next week. Here, 'look into' means to

Options:
a. postpone
b. investigate
c. visit
d. promote


Correct Answer: খ

Solution:
The phrasal verb 'look into' means to investigate or examine something carefully. Therefore 'investigate' is the correct choice.

Explanation:
Phrasal verbs often change the meaning of the base verb. 'Look into' = investigate/enquire into (e.g., The police will look into the case). 'Visit' would match 'call on' or 'pay a visit'; 'postpone' matches 'put off'; 'promote' is unrelated.
