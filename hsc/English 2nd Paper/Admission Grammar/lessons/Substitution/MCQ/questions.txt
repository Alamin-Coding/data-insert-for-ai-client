Question:
A book written about another person's life by someone else is called

Options:
a. an autobiography
b. a biography
c. a memoir
d. a journal


Correct Answer: b

Solution:
b. a biography — a biography is an account of a person's life written by another person. An autobiography is written by the subject about themselves, a memoir focuses on selected personal experiences, and a journal is a regular day-to-day record.

Explanation:
Biography: a detailed description of someone’s life written by someone else (example: 'Steve Jobs' by Walter Isaacson). Autobiography: the subject writes their own life story (example: 'Long Walk to Freedom' by Nelson Mandela). Memoir: a personal recollection concentrating on particular events or periods rather than a full life history (example: 'Eat, Pray, Love' by Elizabeth Gilbert). Journal: chronological daily entries or records kept by an individual (example: 'The Diary of a Young Girl' by Anne Frank). Hence the correct term for a life account written by another person is 'biography'.

Question:
A person who habitually avoids work and prefers idleness

Options:
a. industrious
b. idler
c. diligent
d. assiduous


Correct Answer: b

Solution:
An 'idler' is someone who habitually avoids work and spends time in idleness; therefore option b is correct.

Explanation:
Idler (noun) means a person who is lazy or avoids work. Industrious, diligent, and assiduous all describe someone who works hard and is industrious—opposite in meaning to idler.

Correct Answer: ক/a/ا

Solution:

Explanation:

Question:
Which of the following best describes a syllable?

Options:
a. A part of speech
b. A unit of sound in a word containing a single vowel sound
c. A type of punctuation
d. A complete sentence


Correct Answer: b

Solution:
A syllable is a unit of pronunciation in a word that typically contains one vowel sound and is spoken as a single beat.

Explanation:
A syllable is a part of a word that includes one vowel sound and is pronounced as one unit. For example, 'book' has one syllable, 'reading' has two syllables (read-ing), and 'syllable' has three syllables (syl-la-ble). Syllables can include consonants before or after the vowel, but the defining feature is the single vowel sound pronounced as a unit, so option b is correct.

Question:
The expression 'Habeas Corpus' means –

Options:
ক/a/ا. you shall have the body (produce the person before the court)
খ/b/ب. to tell the truth
গ/c/ج. guilty beyond reasonable doubt
ঘ/d/ث. to dismiss the case


Correct Answer: ক/a/ا

Solution:
The correct answer is 'you shall have the body (produce the person before the court)'. Habeas Corpus is a Latin writ requiring a person under arrest to be brought before a judge.

Explanation:
'Habeas Corpus' is a Latin term literally meaning 'you shall have the body.' It is a legal remedy used to challenge unlawful detention by ordering the custodian to bring the detained person before the court so the legality of the detention can be examined. It does not mean 'to tell the truth' or relate to proof standards like 'guilty beyond reasonable doubt.'

Options:

b. epithet
c. epitaph
d. epitome


Correct Answer: a

Solution:
The correct answer is epigram. An epigram is a brief, witty, and memorable statement or poem.

Explanation:

The people who walk immediately behind a coffin during a funeral procession are called –

a. pallbearers
b. mourners
c. undertakers
d. carers


Correct Answer: b

Solution:
