Which of the following is a primary epic?

c. The Faerie Queene
d. The Epic of Gilgamesh


Correct Answer: d

Solution:
Primary epics are works that originate in oral tradition and collective memory, featuring a heroic central figure and reflecting the values of a culture. The Epic of Gilgamesh is an ancient Mesopotamian poem that developed from oral traditions and is therefore a primary epic. The Aeneid and Paradise Lost are composed literary works (secondary epics), and The Faerie Queene is a Renaissance allegorical epic.

Explanation:
Primary epics (also called folk or oral epics) arise from communal oral composition and transmission (examples: Iliad, Odyssey, Beowulf, Epic of Gilgamesh). Secondary or literary epics are consciously crafted by an individual author working in a literate tradition (examples: Virgil's Aeneid, Milton's Paradise Lost, Spenser's The Faerie Queene). Because Gilgamesh originated in ancient oral-poetic tradition, it qualifies as a primary epic.
