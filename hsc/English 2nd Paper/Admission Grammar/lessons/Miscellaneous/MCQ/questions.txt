Question #200 (ID: 549680)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Question:
Night --------------, the children went to sleep.

Options:
a. falling
b. having fallen
c. being fallen
d. fell


Correct Answer: খ

Solution:
Nominal absolute (Night) + perfect participle (having fallen) + main clause. 'Night having fallen' properly indicates that once night had come, the children went to sleep.

Explanation:
'Having fallen' is the perfect participle used to form a nominative absolute showing that the event of nightfall was completed before the children went to sleep. 'Falling' would be a concurrent action, 'being fallen' is ungrammatical, and 'fell' is a finite verb that doesn't create an absolute construction.
