Eyewitnesses report that the boat, overcrowded with passengers, met with an unfortunate fate causing it to overturn. It follows the inverted-pyramid news structure and journalistic principles (clear, neutral tone; precise numbers; attribution to rescuers/authorities), which makes it fit a ~150-word local-daily brief. The Padma Multipurpose Bridge/The Padma Bridge

The Padma Multipurpose Bridge is a multipurpose road-rail bridge across the Padma River. Bangladesh has constructed the bridge with its own resources. It connects Louhajong, Munshiganj to Shariatpur and Madaripur. Thus, it links the south-west of the country to northern and eastern regions. Actually, it has connected 21 southern districts with the capital. The bridge is a two-level steel truss bridge that carries a four-lane highway on the upper level and a single-track railway on the lower level. Having 41 spans, each 150 m long and 204 steel piles and 32 vertical bored concrete piles, the bridge is 6.15 km long and 18.18m wide. The rail viaduct has 14 spans of 332 m connecting Mawa end to. Jajira end. Apart from transportation, there are other utilities like gas transmission line, optical fiber and telephone line and high voltage electric line through the bridge. The bridge has been constructed by China Railway Major Bridge Engineering Company Limited. The project has taken 1062 hectares of land. Starting on 7. December 2014, the construction ended in June 2022. This dream bridge was inaugurated on June 25, 2022 and was opened for vehicles the following day. The Padma Bridge is the most expensive project in the history of Bangladesh. However, the bridge will contribute a lot to the economic development of Bangladesh. It will increase the GDP by as much as 1.2 percent. Thus, the Padma Multipurpose Bridge is a significant milestone in the construction history of Bangladesh.

Explanation:
The Padma Multipurpose Bridge is a landmark road-rail bridge across the Padma River in Bangladesh that links the country’s southwestern region more directly with Dhaka and the rest of the nation. At about 6.15 kilometres long, the bridge carries a four-lane highway with provision for a railway line and utility corridors, enabling faster, safer transport of people and goods. Fully financed and built by Bangladesh, it was inaugurated in June 2022 and is celebrated as a symbol of national self-reliance. The bridge’s practical benefits include sharply reduced travel time and transport costs, better market access for agricultural and industrial products, increased investment and job opportunities in the southwest, and easier access to health, education and administrative services. Correct Answer: 

Solution:
a Blended learning is an instructional approach that intentionally combines face-to-face classroom activities with asynchronous online resources and tasks. Three pedagogical advantages: (1) Flexibility — students can review digital materials at their own pace, reinforcing in-person instruction. (2) Active learning — in-person time can focus on discussion, problem-solving, and feedback rather than passive lectures. (3) Differentiation — instructors can use online modules to provide remediation or enrichment tailored to individual needs.

b Three potential drawbacks of fully online synchronous classes: (1) Reduced interaction quality — video conferencing limits nonverbal cues and spontaneous peer interactions, making rich discussion harder. (2) Access and equity issues — students with poor bandwidth, noisy environments, or time-zone conflicts may be disadvantaged. (3) Zoom fatigue and attention decline — long live sessions can cause tiredness and lower sustained engagement, hurting learning retention.

c Recommendation: Blended learning is preferable for a hands-on laboratory course. Two reasons: (1) Safety and skill practice — in-person lab sessions are essential for supervised hands-on techniques, equipment handling, and immediate corrective feedback. (2) Efficient use of time — online pre-lab modules (videos, quizzes, protocols) can prepare students so in-lab time is focused on experiments and troubleshooting, improving learning efficiency.

d 6-point implementation plan:
1. Create concise pre-class online modules (videos, readings, quiz) covering theory and safety.  
2. Schedule short in-person lab sessions for hands-on experiments and instructor supervision.  
3. Hold weekly live synchronous Q&A/tutorials to address complex topics and assess misconceptions.  
4. Use LMS discussion boards and peer-review assignments for ongoing collaboration and reflection.  
5. Employ low-stakes online quizzes and practical rubrics to monitor progress and provide timely feedback.  
6. Ensure accessibility by providing recorded sessions, alternative formats, and flexible scheduling options.

Explanation:
The solution distinguishes blended learning from fully synchronous online delivery by focusing on how time and activities are allocated. Blended learning leverages asynchronous materials to free face-to-face time for interactive, higher-order activities — a benefit for differentiation and active learning. Fully synchronous online classes can approximate live interaction but suffer from technical, cognitive, and equity limitations (bandwidth, time zones, fatigue). For laboratory courses, physical presence is critical for developing procedural competence and maintaining safety; blended models combine pre-lab preparation with supervised practice to maximize effectiveness. The 6-point plan operationalizes this: prepare students online, concentrate practical work in person, supplement with live tutorials, maintain continuous engagement via forums, use assessments for feedback, and provide accessibility measures. These steps balance pedagogical goals, logistics, and student support to improve outcomes across diverse contexts.
