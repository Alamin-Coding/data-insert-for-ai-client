Question #1 (ID: 209952)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Type: CQ | Book: English 2nd Paper | Years: 2009 | Boards: Rajshahi Board | Tags: Rajshahi Board
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Question:
(a) The poor man is too hungry to——---.

(b) I came here with a view to——----.

(c) The weather was so cold that——----.

(d) Hasib behaves as if——-----.

(e) The higher we fly, the harder——-----.

Options:


Correct Answer: 

Solution:


Explanation:
(a) The poor man is too hungry to work.  
Explanation: "Too ... to" shows that hunger prevents an action; after "too ... to" we use the infinitive (here "to work") to state the action he cannot do.

(b) I came here with a view to helping you.  
Explanation: In the phrase "with a view to," the word "to" is part of the prepositional phrase, so it must be followed by a gerund (‑ing form), not an infinitive.

(c) The weather was so cold that we couldn't go out.  
Explanation: "So ... that" expresses cause and result; the main clause gives the degree ("so cold") and the "that" clause shows the result or consequence.

(d) Hasib behaves as if he were the boss.  
Explanation: After "as if" we use a past tense (or subjunctive "were") when the situation is unreal or hypothetical, showing he is not actually the boss.

(e) The higher we fly, the harder we fall.  
Explanation: This uses correlative comparatives: "the + comparative, the + comparative" to show that as one thing increases, the other changes accordingly; here it’s also an idiom meaning greater success/risk can lead to a greater fall.

Question:
(a) Water is so vital of all natural resources that-------.

(b) It is water which-------.

(c) Our agriculture which--------.

(d) Though Bangladesh is blessed with rivers–------.

(e) If the rainfall is timely— -----.

Options:


Correct Answer: 

Solution:


Explanation:
(a) Water is vital because it is essential for all living organisms, for crops, industry and daily use; without adequate water life and economies cannot function.  

(b) It is water which sustains physiological processes and plant growth — it transports nutrients, enables photosynthesis and regulates temperature, so life depends on it.  

(c) Our agriculture depends heavily on water for sowing, growth and maturation of crops; insufficient or irregular water directly reduces yields.  

(d) Though Bangladesh is blessed with rivers, seasonal variability, flooding, pollution and poor management mean the country still faces both water-related disasters and dry-season shortages.  

(e) If the rainfall is timely, soil moisture meets crops’ needs at critical stages and yields are good; untimely or insufficient rain causes drought stress or crop failure.

Question:
(a) United we stand,_____.

(b)_____know this wise saying?

(c) Unless you are united, you_____.

(d) The story of the old man and his sons teaches us_____.

(e) It is high time we______.

Options:


Correct Answer: 

Solution:


Explanation:
(a) "divided we fall." — This completes the proverb "United we stand, divided we fall," contrasting strength in unity with weakness in division.

(b) "Do you know this wise saying?" — "Do you know" correctly forms a present simple question asking whether someone is aware of the proverb.

(c) "you will fall." — With "Unless" a negative condition is followed by a future result, so "you will fall" matches the proverb's warning about the consequence of disunity.

(d) "that unity is strength." — The story shows separate sticks break easily but together are unbreakable, so its moral is that unity gives strength.

(e) "we united." (as in "It is high time we united.") — "It is high time" takes a past/subjunctive form to express urgency, so "we united" correctly means we should become united now.

Question #8 (ID: 209931)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Type: CQ | Book: English 2nd Paper | Years: 2012 | Boards: Barisal Board | Tags: Barisal Board
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Question:
(a) He hurried to the station lest,———-

(b) People are advised to take oral-saline since ———–

(c) Where there is a will ———-.

(d) The weather was so unbearable that ———-.

(e) When the final hour of departure came, ———–.

Options:


Correct Answer: 

Solution:


Explanation:
(a) He hurried to the station lest he should miss the train.
Explanation: "Lest" expresses fear of something happening and is followed by a negative idea, commonly using "should" or the subjunctive, so "lest he should miss the train" is grammatical and shows the reason for hurrying.

(b) People are advised to take oral-saline since it prevents dehydration.
Explanation: "Since" gives a reason; oral saline is recommended because it prevents or treats dehydration, so the clause must state that preventive effect.

(c) Where there is a will there is a way.
Explanation: This is a proverb meaning determination produces solutions; the idiomatic completion is "there is a way."

(d) The weather was so unbearable that we had to stay indoors.
Explanation: The "so... that" structure links degree to result; "so unbearable that" requires a result clause (e.g., "we had to stay indoors").

(e) When the final hour of departure came, he bade them farewell.
Explanation: The time clause sets a past moment, so the main clause uses the past verb "bade" (past of "bid") meaning "said goodbye," which completes the sense.

Options:


Correct Answer:

(e) The higher we fly, the harder it is to breathe.
Explanation: Correlative comparatives ("the ... the ...") link degree and result; the second clause must show the increasing effect, here difficulty in breathing.
