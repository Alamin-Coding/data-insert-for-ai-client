Question:
What can be the long-term impact on victims of bullying according to studies?

Options:
a. Increased likelihood of academic success
b. Development of strong social networks
c. Higher chances of mental health issues
d. Greater resilience and coping skills


Correct Answer: গ

Solution:
Step 1: Identify what large-scale studies on bullying typically measure — long-term outcomes such as psychological well‑being, depression, anxiety, and suicidal ideation.  
Step 2: Compare each option to that evidence: option c (higher chances of mental health issues) directly matches repeated findings linking bullying victimization to increased risk of depression, anxiety, and other mental-health problems.  
Step 3: Eliminate the others: option a (increased academic success) and option b (development of strong social networks) contradict typical study results, and option d (greater resilience) may be true for a minority but is not the predominant long-term outcome.  
Therefore the correct answer is c.
