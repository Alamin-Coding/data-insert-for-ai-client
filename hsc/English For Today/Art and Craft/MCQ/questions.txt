সমাধান: Baul একটি মিস্টিক ভ্রমণশীল গীতিকবিতার পরিমণ্ডল যেখানে গান গাওয়া ও আধ্যাত্মিক অভিজ্ঞতার মধ্য দিয়ে তত্ত্ব বিতরণ করা হয়; সেই গানগুলোকেই Baul গান বলা হয়। তাই প্রশ্নে দেওয়া বিকল্পগুলোর মধ্যে গ (Baul) সঠিক। অন্যগুলো ভুল কারণ: ক Bhatiyali নৌকাবিহারী গান, খ Gambhira একটি লোকনৃত্য/লোকনাট্য-ভিত্তিক প্রচলিত রূপ এবং ঘ Murshidi প্রধানত সুফি আধ্যাত্মিক বা মুর্শিদ-সম্পর্কিত ভক্তিমূলক গান।

Question:
What is a characteristic of folk music in Bangladesh?

Options:
a. It is influenced by classical music
b. It is composed by rural folk
c. It requires formal training
d. It is performed only by professionals


Correct Answer: খ

Solution:
Solution:
Step 1: Define folk music — it is traditional music that arises within rural or local communities, passed down orally and reflecting everyday life and culture.  
Step 2: Compare options with that definition: option b states it is composed by rural folk, which matches the origin and creators of folk music; option a (influenced by classical music) can occur but is not a defining characteristic; option c (requires formal training) is false because folk music is typically learned informally; option d (performed only by professionals) is false since amateurs and community members commonly perform it.  
Therefore the correct answer is b.

Question:
In which poem does Emily Dickinson suggest that beauty and truth are inseparable?

Options:
a. She Walks in Beauty
b. I Died For Beauty
c. The Road Not Taken
d. Ode to a Nightingale


Correct Answer: খ

Solution:
Solution:
Emily Dickinson’s poem "I Died for Beauty" explicitly pairs the ideals of Beauty and Truth: the speakers say they died for Beauty and for Truth and are laid in adjoining tombs, speaking like kin, which suggests the two are intimately connected and inseparable. This direct dramatization of Beauty and Truth as companion causes of sacrifice makes option (b) the correct choice. 

Why the others are incorrect:
- a: "She Walks in Beauty" is by Lord Byron and celebrates a woman’s outward and inner grace, but does not present Beauty and Truth as inseparable in the same way.  
- c: "The Road Not Taken" (Robert Frost) deals with choice and consequence, not the Beauty–Truth connection.  
- d: "Ode to a Nightingale" (John Keats) explores beauty, mortality, and escapism, but does not assert that beauty and truth are inseparable.
